Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat largest and most famous temple complex. Built in the 12th century, it began as a Hindu temple and later became a Buddhist site. Known for its grand architecture, intricate carvings, and five-tower design, it’s a UNESCO World Heritage site and popular for sunrise views.

Ta Prohm Temple

Ta Prohm Temple is famous for its unique fusion of ancient ruins and nature. Built in the late 12th century, the temple is enveloped by massive tree roots, creating a mystical, overgrown look.

Ktom Sre Khmer Food

No 10, Ph Srah Srong Tboung Srah Srang South, Siem Reap

Open Hours: Mon-Sun 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM
Ktom Sre Khmer is deeply rooted in the rural lifestyle of Cambodia, where people rely on locally sourced ingredients. The dish reflects the ingenuity of Cambodian villagers who make the most of what is available, turning simple ingredients into something delicious and nourishing.

Open Hours: Mon-Sun 9:00 AM - 11:00 PM
Offering a true taste of traditional Cambodian (Khmer) cuisine in a warm and welcoming setting. Established in the heart of the city, near the lively Pub Street area, Khmer Kitchen has become a go-to destination for both locals and tourists seeking an authentic culinary experience.
